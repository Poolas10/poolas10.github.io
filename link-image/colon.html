Hereditary GI Cancer Surveillance
Syndrome Genes Colorectal / Lower GI Upper GI / Other GI Extraintestinal / Other
Lynch Syndrome MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM
  • Colonoscopy every 1–2 years starting age 20–25
  • Annual colonoscopy for confirmed mutation carriers
  • Baseline EGD with gastric biopsy at age 30–35
  • Treat H. pylori if found
  • EGD every 3–5 years if family history of gastric/duodenal cancer
  • Endometrial biopsy + transvaginal ultrasound annually starting age 30–35
  • Consider hysterectomy + BSO at age 40–45 after childbearing
  • No routine urinary tract, pancreas, prostate, or breast surveillance beyond population guidelines unless family history
Syndrome Genes Colorectal / Lower GI Upper GI / Other GI Extraintestinal / Other
FAP (Classic) APC
  • Annual flexible sigmoidoscopy or colonoscopy starting at puberty
  • Colectomy when polyp burden warrants
  • Upper endoscopy + duodenoscopy starting age 25–30
  • Interval by Spigelman stage: 0=4 yr; I=2–3 yr; II=1–3 yr; III=6–12 mo; IV=surgical evaluation
  • Random biopsy of fundic gland polyps; surgery for high-grade dysplasia
  • Annual thyroid ultrasound
  • Biannual AFP + ultrasound for hepatoblastoma until age 7
  • Desmoid surveillance based on symptoms/family history
AFAP APC
  • Colonoscopy starting at puberty or age 18–20
  • Annual surveillance
  • Upper endoscopy + duodenoscopy starting age 25–30
  • Interval per Spigelman stage
  • Annual thyroid ultrasound
MAP MUTYH
  • Colonoscopy starting at puberty or age 18–25
  • Annual surveillance
  • Upper endoscopy + duodenoscopy starting age 25–30
  • Interval per Spigelman stage
  • Annual thyroid ultrasound
Syndrome Genes Colorectal / Lower GI Upper GI / Other GI Extraintestinal / Other
Peutz-Jeghers (PJS) STK11
  • Colonoscopy every 2–3 years starting age 8
  • If no polyps at age 8, repeat at 18 then every 3 years
  • Upper endoscopy every 2–3 years starting age 8
  • Small bowel capsule endoscopy or MR enterography every 2–3 years starting age 8
  • Annual pancreatic MRI/EUS starting age 30–35
  • Annual breast MRI + mammography starting age 25
  • Annual pelvic exam; gynecologic surveillance
  • Annual testicular exam in males
Juvenile Polyposis (JPS) SMAD4, BMPR1A
  • Colonoscopy every 1–3 years starting age 15 or at diagnosis
  • Polypectomy; colectomy if unmanageable
  • Upper endoscopy starting at diagnosis
  • Repeat every 1–3 years
  • SMAD4 carriers: cardiovascular evaluation for HHT
Cowden Syndrome PTEN
  • Colonoscopy every 1–3 years starting age 35
  • Upper endoscopy every 1–3 years
  • Annual thyroid ultrasound starting age 18
  • Annual breast MRI + mammography starting age 30–35
  • Annual endometrial biopsy or ultrasound starting age 30–35
  • Renal imaging every 1–2 years starting age 40
  • Annual skin exam

Serrated Polyposis (SPS) Unknown; consider MUTYH

  • Surgery if unable to control endoscopically or cancer develops

Syndrome Genes Colorectal / Lower GI Upper GI / Other GI Extraintestinal / Other
Familial Pancreatic Cancer BRCA1/2, CDKN2A, PALB2, ATM, STK11, others
  • Standard CRC screening unless syndrome-specific indications apply
  • Pancreatic surveillance (EUS and/or MRI/MRCP) at experienced centers, ideally under research protocol
  • Indicated for known mutation carriers (PJS, FAMMM, hereditary pancreatitis) or familial kindreds with affected first-degree relative
  • BRCA1/2: Breast and ovarian cancer surveillance per HBOC guidelines
  • CDKN2A (FAMMM): Annual skin exam by dermatologist
Syndrome Genes Colorectal / Lower GI Upper GI / Other GI Extraintestinal / Other
HDGC CDH1
  • Colonoscopy starting age 40 in families with colon cancer history
  • Prophylactic total gastrectomy recommended after age 20 (>80% lifetime risk)
  • If deferred: annual EGD with Cambridge protocol (minimum 30 random biopsies)
  • Annual mammography + breast MRI starting age 35
  • Clinical breast exam every 6 months
Surveillance table adapted from Table 10 in: Syngal S et al. ACG clinical guideline: genetic testing and management of hereditary GI cancer syndromes. Am J Gastroenterol. 2015;110(2):223-62. doi: 10.1038/ajg.2014.435. For clinical decisions, always consult the original guideline and current updates.
